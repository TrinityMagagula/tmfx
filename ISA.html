INDEPENDENT SALES AGENT AGREEMENT

This Independent Sales Agent Agreement ("Agreement") is made and entered into on [Insert Date] by and between:

TM SALES AND MARKETING (Pty) Ltd, a company incorporated in South Africa with Registration Number: [Insert Number], and with its principal place of business at [Insert Address] ("the Company");

and

[Agent Full Name], Identity/Passport Number: [Insert ID Number], residing at [Insert Residential Address] ("the Independent Sales Agent" or "ISA").

1. Appointment and Scope

1.1 The Company appoints the ISA to promote and sell approved products and services of TM FX TRADING (Pty) Ltd under the Company's supervision and direction.

1.2 The ISA shall operate within South Africa and only engage in territories authorized by the Company.

1.3 The ISA shall not act as an employee, representative, or financial advisor of the Client.

1.4 This is a non-exclusive agreement; the Company may engage multiple agents simultaneously and assign or withdraw campaigns at its discretion.

1.5 Nothing in this Agreement shall be construed as guaranteeing a minimum term of engagement or any right to continued campaigns.

2. Independent Contractor Status

2.1 The ISA is engaged as an independent contractor. Nothing in this Agreement shall be construed as creating an employment, partnership, or agency relationship between the Company and the ISA.

2.2 The ISA is responsible for their own tax filings, operational expenses, and compliance with SARS and other statutory bodies.

2.3 No employee benefits (UIF, pension, sick leave, annual leave) apply under this Agreement.

3. Remuneration and Commission

3.1 The ISA shall earn 25% commission on the gross invoice amount received by the Client, excluding VAT, transaction fees, and refunds.

3.2 Commissions are only payable on:

  • Fully paid and confirmed sales;
  • Transactions not subject to cancellation, fraud, or dispute;
  • Sales properly recorded via the Company's CRM or other tracking systems.

3.3 Commissions will be paid weekly, net 7 days, following receipt and confirmation of payment from the Client.

3.4 The ISA is required to submit commission statements or activity reports as prescribed in Company SOPs.

3.5 No base salary, allowance, or advance is provided unless explicitly stated in writing.

3.6 Commission is capped at the maximum specified in any campaign-specific addendum and is subject to revision with 7 days' notice.

3.7 If a sale is later cancelled, refunded, or determined to be fraudulent, the associated commission shall be reversed or deducted from future payouts.

3.8 Only commissions earned and recorded before the date of termination, for transactions that remain verified and valid, shall be payable. No further earnings shall accrue post-termination.

4. Conduct and Compliance

4.1 The ISA shall:

  • Represent products truthfully and transparently;
  • Wear or present valid identification as required;
  • Operate professionally and ethically at all times;
  • Not offer, solicit, or accept any form of bribe, inducement, or unauthorized incentive;
  • Disclose all conflicts of interest in writing.

4.2 The ISA may not:

  • Provide financial or investment advice (as per FAIS Act);
  • Guarantee income or performance of products;
  • Use misleading or high-pressure tactics;
  • Violate the CPA, POPIA, or any other regulatory legislation;
  • Engage with individuals, companies, or jurisdictions subject to international sanctions or regulatory restrictions, including those issued by the UN, OFAC, or the EU.

4.3 The ISA agrees to abide by the Company's Code of Conduct, attached as Annexure A, and attend mandatory training sessions when required.

4.4 The ISA confirms and agrees to operate on an execution-only basis. The ISA shall not render any financial advice, compare financial products, or offer opinions on account performance or investment returns. All such enquiries must be directed to TM FX TRADING’s authorised support channels.

4.5 Campaign-specific terms, such as commission adjustments, KPIs, lead assignment, or training requirements, may be issued by the Company in writing via Addenda or SOPs. These shall be binding upon the ISA once communicated.

4.6 The ISA is encouraged to maintain personal liability insurance to cover incidents or claims arising in the course of their engagement, particularly while engaging with customers or representing the Company in the field.

5. Data Protection and Confidentiality

5.1 The ISA shall comply strictly with the Protection of Personal Information Act (POPIA) and handle any customer or lead data securely and confidentially.

5.2 All leads, customer details, contracts, and campaign materials remain the exclusive property of the Company and Client.

5.3 Any data breach or suspected incident must be reported to the Company within 24 hours.

6. Non-Circumvention

6.1 The ISA agrees not to bypass or attempt to contract directly with any client, lead, or prospect introduced via the Company for a period of 12 months following the termination of this Agreement.

6.2 Breach of this clause will result in immediate termination and may lead to civil legal action.

7. Branding and Intellectual Property

7.1 The ISA may use the Company's marketing materials and logos only for the purposes of authorized campaigns.

7.2 The ISA shall not copy, alter, or distribute brand assets outside the approved scope.

7.3 7.3 All digital platforms, CRM systems, and software tools provided to the ISA by the Company remain the exclusive property of the Company. The Company reserves the right to revoke access at any time and to monitor usage for compliance and performance.

8. Monitoring and Reporting

8.1 The Company may monitor ISA sales performance, client feedback, and compliance with procedures.

8.2 The ISA must maintain clear records of all sales, client interactions, and marketing efforts, and submit reports as required.

9. Disciplinary Action and Termination

9.1 This Agreement may be terminated:

  • By either party with 7 days' written notice;
  • Immediately by the Company in cases of:
    • Fraud, misrepresentation, or non-compliance;
    • Regulatory breach;
    • Reputational harm caused to the Company or Client;
    • Gross misconduct or criminal activity.

9.2 Where misconduct is not gross or criminal, the Company may issue up to two written warnings before terminating the Agreement.

9.3 On termination, the ISA shall immediately cease all use of Company materials, return customer data, and discontinue sales activity.

9.4 The Company reserves the right to audit the ISA's performance, documentation, and client engagements at any time.

10. Non-Solicitation and Exclusivity

10.1 The ISA agrees not to solicit or contract directly with any client or competitor of the Company for a period of 6 months following termination.

10.2 All client relationships and sales pipelines developed during the term remain the property of the Company.

10.3 The ISA shall not make any public or private statements, whether online or offline, that disparage, defame, or otherwise harm the reputation of the Company, the Client, or any associated campaigns during the term of this Agreement or for a period of 12 months after its termination.

11. Dispute Resolution

11.1 Any dispute arising from this Agreement shall first be attempted to be resolved through internal resolution.

11.2 Failing that, the matter shall be referred to mediation, and if unresolved, to binding arbitration under the Arbitration Foundation of Southern Africa (AFSA).

12. Force Majeure

12.1 Neither party shall be liable for failure to perform its obligations due to acts beyond its control, including but not limited to natural disasters, civil unrest, or regulatory changes.

13. General Provisions

13.1 This Agreement, including its annexures, constitutes the entire understanding between the parties and supersedes all prior discussions or representations.

13.2 Any amendments must be in writing and signed (physically or digitally) by both parties.

13.3 The Agreement shall be governed by the laws of the Republic of South Africa, and any legal proceedings shall be conducted in a competent court within the Gauteng Province.

13.4 Both parties agree that digitally executed signatures, such as those affixed via PDF, DocuSign, or similar, are legally binding when compliant with Section 13 of the Electronic Communications and Transactions Act (ECTA).

13.5 The ISA shall indemnify the Company against any loss, claim, or liability arising from breach of this Agreement or any misrepresentation made in the course of their sales activity.

ANNEXURE A
Code of Conduct & Compliance Checklist

This Code applies to all Independent Sales Agents ("ISAs") contracted under TM SALES AND MARKETING (Pty) Ltd and is binding throughout the duration of their engagement.

A. Professional Conduct

  • Always act in a manner that reflects positively on TM SALES AND MARKETING and TM FX TRADING.
  • Wear presentable attire and, where applicable, official identification badges.
  • Use only approved sales scripts and marketing material.
  • Never pressure or mislead a customer to make a purchase.
  • Represent all product pricing, benefits, risks, and terms transparently.
  • Do not consume alcohol or drugs during work hours or while engaging with clients.

B. Legal & Regulatory Compliance

  • Do not provide financial advice or investment opinions (in accordance with the FAIS Act).
  • Adhere strictly to POPIA and CPA regulations.
  • Ensure all personal data is securely stored or submitted through official channels (e.g., CRM, encrypted documents).
  • Never collect card details, ID copies, or passwords unless instructed and authorized by TM SALES AND MARKETING.
  • Confirmed understanding of the execution-only sales model and the prohibition of financial advice under FAIS
  • Inform clients verbally and in writing of their right to cancel within 5 business days as per Section 16 of the Consumer Protection Act.

C. Discipline

Violations of this Code may result in:

  • Written warnings (up to two warnings for non-gross misconduct)
  • Suspension from campaigns
  • Immediate termination of the Agreement for serious breaches
  • Legal or regulatory action where applicable

ANNEXURE C
POPIA Compliance Guide for Independent Agents

This guide outlines how Independent Sales Agents must comply with the Protection of Personal Information Act, 2013 (POPIA).

1. Data Collection

  • Collect only the minimum necessary personal information.
  • Inform clients that data is collected on behalf of TM SALES AND MARKETING and TM FX TRADING.
  • Get clear consent for processing data when required.

2. Data Handling

  • Store all data using the official CRM or secured company systems.
  • Never store customer data on personal devices.
  • Do not send client info over WhatsApp, SMS, or personal email.

3. Data Sharing

  • Only authorized personnel may access or share customer data.
  • Do not share data with friends, family, or other agents.

4. Data Breach Protocol

  • If a device is lost, hacked, or customer data is leaked, notify the Company within 24 hours.
  • Cooperate fully in any internal or external investigations.

5. Escalations or Referrals

  • If a customer requests advice on trading strategies, results, or financial products, the ISA shall direct the customer to TM FX TRADING’s support line or website. No opinions or advice may be given.

6. Consequences

  • Breach of POPIA may result in:
    • Termination of contract
    • Personal liability under South African law
    • Legal action by affected customers or authorities
