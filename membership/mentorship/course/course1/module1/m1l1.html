Lesson 1: What is the Forex Market?

üåç What is the Forex Market?

The foreign exchange market, also known as Forex or FX, is the global marketplace for trading national currencies against one another.

It is the largest and most liquid financial market in the world, with a daily trading volume exceeding $7 trillion as of 2023. This means trillions of dollars are exchanged every day, involving banks, governments, businesses, investors, and traders from around the world.

Unlike stock markets, the Forex market has no centralized location. It is conducted electronically over-the-counter (OTC), meaning all transactions occur via computer networks between traders globally.

üï∞Ô∏è A Brief History of the Forex Market

Foreign exchange has existed for thousands of years ‚Äî ever since people needed to exchange currencies to buy goods and services from other regions. But the modern Forex market began after the collapse of the Bretton Woods Agreement in the early 1970s.

  • 1944: The Bretton Woods system was established to stabilize currency values, pegging them to gold.
  • 1971: The U.S. abandoned the gold standard, causing the fixed currency exchange system to collapse.
  • 1973: Currencies began to float freely against one another ‚Äî the birth of the modern Forex market.

Initially, Forex trading was only accessible to large institutions and governments. But with the rise of the internet and technology in the 1990s and 2000s, retail traders (like you) gained access through online brokers.

Example: In the past, only big banks could trade currencies. Today, with as little as $10 and a smartphone, almost anyone can open a trading account and participate.

üí± How It Works

When you trade Forex, you're always buying one currency and selling another ‚Äî this is called a currency pair.

Example: If you're trading the pair EUR/USD and you buy, you're buying Euros and selling US Dollars. If EUR increases in value compared to USD, you profit.

üìà Major Currency Pairs

  • EUR/USD ‚Äì Euro / US Dollar
  • GBP/USD ‚Äì British Pound / US Dollar
  • USD/JPY ‚Äì US Dollar / Japanese Yen
  • USD/CHF ‚Äì US Dollar / Swiss Franc
  • AUD/USD ‚Äì Australian Dollar / US Dollar

These are called major pairs and are the most traded in the world due to their high liquidity and low spreads.

üïí Forex Market Hours

The Forex market is open 24 hours a day, 5 days a week ‚Äî from Monday to Friday.

It operates in 4 main trading sessions:

  • London Session: 8 AM ‚Äì 4 PM GMT
  • New York Session: 1 PM ‚Äì 9 PM GMT
  • Tokyo Session: 12 AM ‚Äì 8 AM GMT
  • Sydney Session: 10 PM ‚Äì 6 AM GMT
Tip: The best time to trade is when two sessions overlap, like London + New York (1 PM ‚Äì 4 PM GMT) because of high volatility and volume.

üßÆ Forex Market Participants

Understanding who trades in the Forex market helps you grasp why prices move the way they do:

  • Central Banks: Control national monetary policies and stabilize currency value.
  • Commercial Banks: Facilitate transactions for clients and speculate on currency movements.
  • Hedge Funds & Investment Firms: Trade large volumes based on macroeconomic theories.
  • Corporations: Exchange currencies for international business deals and hedging.
  • Retail Traders: Individual traders using online platforms for speculation and profit.
