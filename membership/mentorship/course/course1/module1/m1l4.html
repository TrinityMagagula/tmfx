Lesson 4: How Currency Pairs Are Quoted (Base/Quote)

üí± What Is a Currency Quote?

In the Forex market, currencies are always quoted in pairs. This is because every trade involves buying one currency and selling another at the same time. Each currency in the pair is represented by a three-letter code, based on the ISO standard (like USD for US Dollar or EUR for Euro).

  • Base Currency ‚Äì the first currency in the pair (on the left).
  • Quote Currency ‚Äì the second currency in the pair (on the right).

The quote tells you how much of the quote currency is needed to purchase one unit of the base currency.

Example: If EUR/USD = 1.1500, it means 1 Euro (EUR) = 1.15 US Dollars (USD).

Think of it like shopping: the base currency is the product, and the quote currency is the price tag.

üßÆ Reading and Understanding a Forex Quote

Let‚Äôs break it down further using different combinations of popular currency pairs:

USD/JPY = 145.25 ‚Üí 1 US Dollar is worth 145.25 Japanese Yen.
GBP/USD = 1.2800 ‚Üí 1 British Pound is worth 1.28 US Dollars.
AUD/CAD = 0.9100 ‚Üí 1 Australian Dollar is worth 0.91 Canadian Dollars.
NZD/USD = 0.6300 ‚Üí 1 New Zealand Dollar is worth 0.63 US Dollars.

Remember: the number you see in a quote tells you the value of 1 unit of the base currency, expressed in the quote currency.

If USD/CHF = 0.8900, it means 1 USD (base) can be exchanged for 0.89 Swiss Francs (quote).

Quotes can also move up or down based on market activity, news, or economic indicators. A change from 1.1000 to 1.1050 in EUR/USD means the Euro is strengthening, and the Dollar is weakening in that relationship.

üìà What Happens When You Buy or Sell a Pair?

Trading Forex means you're predicting whether one currency will go up or down compared to another.

  • Buy (go long) ‚Üí You expect the base currency to rise in value versus the quote.
  • Sell (go short) ‚Üí You expect the base currency to fall in value versus the quote.
Scenario 1: You expect the Euro to strengthen against the US Dollar.
You buy EUR/USD at 1.1000.
If it rises to 1.1200, you profit because your Euro is now worth more Dollars.

Scenario 2: You think the British Pound will weaken against the Dollar.
You sell GBP/USD at 1.2800.
If it falls to 1.2500, the Pound has dropped in value and you profit.

Always remember: when you trade a currency pair, you're making a bet on the direction of the base currency compared to the quote currency.

üîÅ Direct vs Indirect Quotes

Whether a quote is ‚Äúdirect‚Äù or ‚Äúindirect‚Äù depends on your location and your home currency. This helps you interpret the quote in a way that makes sense to you.

  • Direct Quote ‚Äì Your local currency is the quote (right) currency.
  • Indirect Quote ‚Äì Your local currency is the base (left) currency.
In the US:
EUR/USD = 1.1000 ‚Üí Direct quote (shows how many USD per 1 Euro)

In South Africa:
USD/ZAR = 18.50 ‚Üí Direct quote (shows how many Rand for 1 Dollar)

In Europe:
USD/EUR = 0.9090 ‚Üí Indirect quote (shows how many Euros per 1 Dollar)

Traders typically prefer using direct quotes, but most Forex platforms stick to common international formats (like EUR/USD instead of USD/EUR).

üìä Bid, Ask, and Spread

When trading, you‚Äôll see two prices for every currency pair: the bid and the ask.

  • Bid Price ‚Äì The price at which the broker is willing to buy the base currency from you (you sell).
  • Ask Price ‚Äì The price at which the broker is willing to sell the base currency to you (you buy).
  • Spread ‚Äì The small difference between bid and ask. This is how brokers make money.
EUR/USD: 1.1050 / 1.1052 ‚Üí
Bid = 1.1050 | Ask = 1.1052
Spread = 0.0002 (2 pips)

A smaller spread is usually better for traders, especially for scalpers and high-frequency traders.

üåç Cross Currency Pairs (No USD)

A cross currency pair is a pair that does not involve the US Dollar (USD). These pairs can be more volatile and carry higher transaction costs due to lower liquidity.

Examples:
EUR/GBP = 0.8700 ‚Üí 1 Euro = 0.87 British Pounds
AUD/NZD = 1.0750 ‚Üí 1 Australian Dollar = 1.075 New Zealand Dollars
GBP/JPY = 183.20 ‚Üí 1 British Pound = 183.20 Japanese Yen

These pairs are useful when you want to trade two strong currencies directly, without referencing the US Dollar.

üìå How Quotes Affect Strategy

Understanding how quotes work helps in deciding:

  • When to enter or exit a trade
  • Which direction to trade (long or short)
  • How economic news impacts currency values
  • How spreads can eat into profits

It also allows you to manage risk more effectively, especially when using stop-loss and take-profit orders.
