üîπ What is a Lot?

In Forex trading, a lot is the standardized unit of measurement for a trade size. Instead of buying or selling single units of currency, traders use lots to express how much of a currency pair they are trading.

Types of Lots:

  • Standard Lot = 100,000 units of the base currency
  • Mini Lot = 10,000 units
  • Micro Lot = 1,000 units
  • Nano Lot = 100 units (very small, mainly used in cent or demo accounts)

üîπ What is Leverage?

Leverage allows you to control a larger position in the market with a smaller amount of your own capital. It‚Äôs expressed as a ratio ‚Äî such as 1:50, 1:100, or 1:500.

Leverage is a double-edged sword. While it can magnify your profits, it also increases the potential for large losses. Many beginner traders make the mistake of using too much leverage, which can wipe out their accounts quickly.

Why is Leverage Risky?

  • A small movement in price against your position can lead to a large loss.
  • High leverage reduces the margin of error in your trades.
  • Brokers may issue a margin call or automatically close your trades if your losses exceed a certain level.
Example:
Let‚Äôs say you have $100 and open a trade using 1:500 leverage (controlling $50,000).

This is why professional traders use low leverage (e.g., 1:10 or 1:20) and apply strict risk management.
